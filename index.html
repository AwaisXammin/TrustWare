About Us

At TrustWare we believe the decentralized internet is the future of technology. We are committed to empowering humans by providing tools that promote self-sovereignty and freedom. We believe that an inclusive, equitable and secure decentralized future is fundamental for public good.

FAQ

Here are some of our most commonly asked questions.

TrustWare features the same basic wallet layout most of us are used to with a full security feature set under the surface. In 2022 alone over $2B in digital assets have been stolen from users and institutions, mainly through vulnerabilities stemming from traditional wallet systems which rely on single private key setups. With TrustWare, even if one of your private keys is compromised, your assets will remain safe. Simply switching to a TrustWare wallet can protect you from many common threats to digital asset security.

At the core of TrustWare is tried and true cryptographic logic revolving around a simple, yet robust three key system. By proving ownership of two out of the three keys, users can effectively recover or reinitialize the third. This system allows the TrustWare smart contract to verify ownership and “identity” without the need for storage of sensitive data. In our system you are the only party with access to your keys and assets, from wallet creation onwards.

TrustWare is fully EVM compatible and accepts all ERC-20 token standard assets including cryptocurrencies and NFTs. We will soon be adding support for more digital assets and tokens.

Upon full launch, the TrustWare wallet app will be available across all major platforms including iOS, Android and Web. TrustWare Wallet is free to download and requires only an NFT license key to operate. The key is fully transferable and will be available for pre-sale soon. Sign up for our mailing list to stay in the loop on all our latest developments.

Yes, the beauty of TrustWare lies in its optionality and emphasis on user choice. On the most basic level, you can utilize TrustWare like any standard wallet, to send and receive digital assets. TrustWare also allows for other existing wallets such as a Metamask Ledger or Trezor, and security tools such as a YubiKey or other physical 2FA devices to create and store one of your identity or security keys during setup, adding even further security to our stack.

The TrustWare team is a fully doxxed and vetted team of industry veterans determined to empower users. Our patent pending product logic is based on age-old cryptographic standards, and our smart contract is in the process of being fully audited. Unlike competitors, we don’t store user information or participate in the key creation process when users initialize a wallet. We are focused on building self-custody tools for users that promote their ability to maintain control over their own assets.

Most crypto wallets on the market today rely on a single private key system. This means that if a malicious actor gets ahold of that single private key or your seed phrase, there is nothing stopping them from taking everything in your wallet. TrustWare wallets are the first to protect users on both sides, input and output. As we require two out of three keys to confirm transactions, even if a malicious actor were to gain access to your TrustWare wallet, they would still need two unique keys to confirm any transfer of assets out of the wallet. This process makes hacking TrustWare wallets exponentially more difficult, making TrustWare wallet users hard targets.
